# Western Culture

"Western culture" refers broadly to the cultural traditions, values, and institutions rooted in European history and spread globally through colonialism, trade, and (more recently) media. It is now deeply influential worldwide.

---

## Historical Roots

Western culture draws from several streams:

- **Ancient Greece** — democracy, philosophy, logic, art, theater
- **Ancient Rome** — law, governance, engineering, Latin language
- **Christianity** — moral framework, calendar, art, education
- **The Enlightenment** (17th–18th centuries) — reason, science, individual rights, secularism
- **The Industrial Revolution** (18th–19th centuries) — technology, capitalism, urbanization
- **American influence** (20th–21st centuries) — pop culture, technology, military power

---

## Core Values (Broadly)

- **Individualism** — the individual is the basic moral unit; personal freedom and autonomy are highly valued
- **Democracy** — government should represent the people
- **Secular governance** — religion and state are (ideally) separate
- **Rule of law** — everyone is subject to the same laws
- **Scientific rationalism** — empirical evidence is the basis for truth claims
- **Progress** — things can and should improve over time

---

## American Culture (A Specific Example)

The United States is the dominant force in Western culture globally through media and technology.

Key features of American culture:
- **Optimism** — a cultural belief that things can be improved through effort
- **The American Dream** — the idea that anyone can succeed through hard work, regardless of background
- **Direct communication** — saying what you mean is generally valued
- **Customer service culture** — the expectation that service providers will be enthusiastic and friendly
- **Informality** — first names are used quickly; formality is relatively low
- **Sports culture** — American football, basketball, baseball are deeply woven into social life ([[Basketball]])
- **Religion** — unusually religious for a wealthy Western country; ~65% identify as Christian, but enormous diversity

---

## The American Political Landscape

The US has two dominant political parties:

- **Democratic Party** — generally left-of-center; supports government programs, progressive social policies, environmental regulation
- **Republican Party** — generally right-of-center; supports lower taxes, limited government, traditional values, strong military

The country is deeply **politically polarized** — meaning the two sides disagree sharply and talk past each other. This is a source of significant tension.

🚩 *Discussing US politics with people you don't know well can be risky — it can quickly become heated. Read the room.*

---

## European Culture (Brief Overview)

Europe is diverse internally, but some broad patterns:
- Stronger **social safety nets** than the US (healthcare, housing, unemployment)
- More **secular** — religious practice is lower than the US
- **History** is more present — centuries of art, architecture, and conflict surround you
- More **reserved** initially than Americans; formality in titles (Mr., Mrs., professional titles) is more maintained
- **Café culture** and public spaces are important social institutions
- Food and wine are taken very seriously in France, Italy, Spain
