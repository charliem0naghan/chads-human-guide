# Shopping

**Shopping** is the process of browsing and purchasing goods and services in exchange for money. It is a fundamental daily activity for most humans.

---

## What Do Humans Buy?

Virtually everything humans use is purchased rather than made:
- **Food** — from supermarkets, grocery stores, farmers markets, restaurants
- **Clothing** — from clothing stores, department stores, online retailers
- **Housing** — rented or purchased ([[Housing & Shelter]])
- **Electronics** — phones, computers, appliances
- **Services** — healthcare, haircuts, transportation, entertainment

---

## Types of Retail

### Physical Stores
Buildings where goods are displayed and sold:
- **Supermarket / Grocery store** — food, beverages, household basics
- **Department store** — clothing, home goods, cosmetics under one roof
- **Specialty store** — focused on one category (bookstore, electronics store, pharmacy)
- **Convenience store** — small, open long hours, basic items at higher prices
- **Market / Bazaar** — often outdoor; produce, crafts, street food; common globally

### Online Shopping
Purchasing through websites and apps. Goods are shipped to your door.
- **Amazon** — the dominant global platform (especially in the US)
- **Alibaba / Taobao** — dominant in China
- Returns are usually possible within a window of time

---

## How Buying Works

1. Select the item(s)
2. Proceed to **checkout** (cashier or self-checkout)
3. **Pay** — cash, credit/debit card, or mobile payment
4. Receive a **receipt** — a record of the transaction; keep for potential returns

**Prices and taxes:**
- Posted prices in the US often **don't include sales tax** — the final price will be higher
- In most of Europe and elsewhere, the displayed price is the total price

**Bargaining:**
🚩 *In most Western supermarkets and chain stores, prices are fixed — you don't negotiate.*
In many markets across Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East, **bargaining (haggling)** over price is expected and normal. Starting too high and negotiating down is part of the social transaction.

---

## Consumer Culture

Modern economies, especially in the West, are heavily **consumerist** — there is significant cultural pressure to buy more, newer, better things. This drives economic activity but also generates waste, debt, and environmental damage.

**Fast fashion** (cheap, rapidly changing clothing) and **planned obsolescence** (products designed to become outdated quickly) are critiqued for their environmental and social impacts.

---

## Practical Tips for Chad

- Always **compare prices** before committing — especially for larger purchases
- **Keep receipts** in case you need to return something
- Beware of **scams** — if a deal seems too good to be true, it usually is
- **Don't impulse buy** — waiting 24–48 hours before a non-essential purchase often prevents regret
- In restaurants: review the bill before paying; errors occur
