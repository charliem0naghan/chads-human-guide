# Visual Art

**Visual art** is the creation of aesthetic experiences through visual means — painting, sculpture, drawing, photography, film, architecture, and more. Art is a fundamental mode of human expression and communication, capable of conveying what words cannot.

---

## Why Art Exists

Art serves many functions:
- **Expression** — externalizing internal experiences, emotions, and ideas
- **Communication** — conveying meaning across language barriers and time
- **Cultural record** — documenting how people lived, believed, and felt
- **Beauty** — creating pleasure through aesthetics
- **Challenge** — questioning assumptions; provoking thought
- **Ritual** — sacred images, ceremonial objects
- **Status** — art as symbol of wealth, power, or taste

---

## Major Art Forms

| Form | Description |
|---|---|
| **Painting** | Applying pigment (color) to a flat surface |
| **Sculpture** | Three-dimensional works in stone, metal, clay, wood |
| **Drawing** | Lines and marks on a surface |
| **Photography** | Capturing images with a camera |
| **Film / Cinema** | Moving images that tell stories or convey experience ([[Movies & Entertainment]]) |
| **Architecture** | The design of buildings — functional AND aesthetic |
| **Printmaking** | Creating images transferred from a surface (woodblock, etching) |
| **Digital art** | Created using computers and software |
| **Street art** | Created in public spaces; graffiti as art form |
| **Textile / Fiber arts** | Weaving, embroidery, quilting |

## Art History Highlights

Some movements and works Chad should know exist:

- **Renaissance** (1400s–1600s, Italy) — realistic representation; da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael. The Sistine Chapel ceiling; Mona Lisa.
- **Baroque** (1600s) — dramatic light and shadow; emotional intensity; Rembrandt, Caravaggio
- **Impressionism** (1800s, France) — capturing light and momentary experience; Monet, Renoir
- **Modernism** (1900s) — breaking tradition; cubism (Picasso), abstract expressionism (Pollock)
- **Contemporary art** — extremely diverse; anything and everything goes

Key works to be aware of:
- **Mona Lisa** (da Vinci) — most famous painting; in the Louvre, Paris
- **Starry Night** (Van Gogh) — swirling night sky
- **The Scream** (Munch) — anguished figure; widely reproduced
- **Guernica** (Picasso) — anti-war statement; cubist
- **The Birth of Venus** (Botticelli) — classical beauty; Renaissance

## Experiencing Art

Art is meant to be encountered:
- **Museums and galleries** — most major cities have world-class museums (Louvre in Paris, Met in New York, British Museum in London, Uffizi in Florence)
- **Street and public art** — in cities everywhere
- **Online** — Google Arts & Culture provides virtual access to thousands of works

---

## Art and Culture

Every human culture has its own rich artistic traditions:
- **Islamic art** — intricate geometric patterns; calligraphy; tile work (figurative representation avoided)
- **East Asian art** — ink painting; calligraphy; ceramics; ukiyo-e woodblock prints (Japan)
- **African art** — masks, textiles, beadwork; often functional and ceremonial
- **Indigenous art** — deeply connected to land, spirit, and community (Aboriginal dot paintings; Native American pottery; Andean weaving)
